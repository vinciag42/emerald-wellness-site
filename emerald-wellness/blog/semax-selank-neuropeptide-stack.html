Origins: From Soviet Neuroscience to Global Biohacking

Both Semax and Selank were developed by the Institute of Molecular Genetics of the Russian Academy of Sciences — Semax in the 1980s, Selank in the 1990s. They were designed specifically to be stable, non-addictive peptide-based neurological drugs: Semax as a cognitive enhancer and neuroprotective agent, Selank as an anti-anxiety compound without the risks of benzodiazepines.

In Russia, both are registered pharmaceutical drugs available by prescription. In most Western countries they exist in a regulatory gray zone — research chemicals that have accumulated extensive human data through decades of clinical use in Eastern Europe.

They've entered the biohacking mainstream largely through the influence of Dave Asprey, Ben Greenfield, and Silicon Valley performance communities — where their uniquely clean cognitive profiles (no tolerance, no crash, no withdrawal) distinguish them sharply from conventional nootropics and stimulants.

Semax is a heptapeptide analogue of ACTH(4-7) — the cognitively active fragment of adrenocorticotropic hormone — with a Pro-Gly-Pro sequence added to increase stability and CNS penetrance.

Its most significant and well-documented effect is dramatic upregulation of BDNF (Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor) — sometimes called "Miracle-Gro for the brain." BDNF is the primary driver of neuroplasticity: it governs the formation of new synaptic connections, the survival of existing neurons, and the brain's capacity to learn and adapt. BDNF levels decline with age, stress, poor sleep, and sedentary behavior — and this decline correlates strongly with cognitive aging.

Semax also increases dopaminergic tone in the prefrontal cortex — the region governing executive function, working memory, and goal-directed attention. This is the mechanism behind the sharp, clear-headed focus users describe as distinctly different from caffeine or amphetamine-based stimulants.

Selank is a synthetic analogue of tuftsin — an endogenous immunomodulatory tetrapeptide. It modulates the GABAergic system — the same system targeted by benzodiazepines — but through a fundamentally different mechanism that produces anxiolysis without sedation, tolerance, or physical dependence.

This makes it categorically different from pharmaceutical anxiolytics. Users describe a quiet, stable calm — a reduction in background noise and rumination — without any blunting of cognition, motivation, or alertness. In clinical terms, it reduces state anxiety without affecting processing speed or executive function.

Selank also independently upregulates BDNF and modulates enkephalin metabolism — adding a complementary neuroplasticity mechanism that overlaps with Semax's effects and creates meaningful synergy in the stack.

Administration Guide — Intranasal

Both Semax and Selank are most commonly administered intranasally via nasal spray bottle. The nasal mucosa provides rapid absorption into systemic circulation and preferential CNS delivery via the olfactory-trigeminal pathway — bypassing the blood-brain barrier more effectively than most routes.

  1. Prepare solution: Reconstitute lyophilized peptide with bacteriostatic water. Standard concentration: 1mg/mL (1mg peptide in 1mL BAC water) — gives 100mcg per 0.1mL spray.
  2. Use a nasal atomizer: A mucosal atomization device (MAD Nasal) produces fine droplets for better absorption than a standard nasal spray pump.
  3. Tilt head slightly forward, not back. Insert atomizer just inside the nostril, aim slightly toward the outer wall of the nasal passage (away from the septum).
  4. Administer in both nostrils for larger doses — split evenly. For 400mcg: 2 sprays per nostril at 100mcg/spray.
  5. Sniff gently after administration to distribute solution across mucosa. Do not forcefully inhale.
  6. Store nasal spray bottle refrigerated. Reconstituted solution: stable up to 30 days refrigerated.

Safety & Side Effects

Both Semax and Selank have notably clean safety profiles — one of the primary reasons they've gained traction in biohacking communities despite being research peptides:

  • No withdrawal syndrome — unlike benzodiazepines, Selank does not cause physical dependence or rebound anxiety upon discontinuation
  • No stimulant crash — Semax's cognitive activation does not produce the post-dose crash of amphetamine-class compounds
  • No hepatotoxicity — both peptides are cleared renally, not hepatically
  • Most commonly reported side effects: mild nasal irritation from the administration route, occasional mild headache in the first few days, rare mild fatigue
  • Semax may exacerbate anxiety in a small minority of users at high doses — start low and assess

FAQ

Can I use Semax and Selank together in the same nasal spray?

Technically possible but not recommended — you lose the ability to adjust doses independently, and stability data for combined solutions is limited. Use separate vials and administer a few minutes apart.

How quickly will I notice effects?

Semax's cognitive effects are typically felt within 30–60 minutes of intranasal administration. Selank's anxiolytic effect is often noticed within 20–40 minutes. The cumulative neuroplastic effects (improved baseline cognitive function, mood stability) develop over 1–3 weeks of consistent use.

Are these peptides detectable in drug testing?

Standard workplace and athletic drug tests do not screen for Semax or Selank. However, WADA (World Anti-Doping Agency) lists peptides as a prohibited class in some categories — competitive athletes should verify current WADA code applicability before use.

Can I use Semax or Selank long-term?

Long-term human safety data is limited but the available evidence (from decades of clinical use in Russia) is broadly reassuring. Most practitioners recommend cycling protocols (3 weeks on, 1 week off) as a precautionary measure and to prevent adaptation.
