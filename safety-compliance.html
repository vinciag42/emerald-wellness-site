Member FAQ

Questions members often ask before they begin.

Clear expectations help every Emerald Wellness member use the platform with confidence, discretion, and the right provider-supported boundaries.

Does Emerald Wellness replace my doctor?

No. Emerald Wellness does not diagnose, treat, cure, prevent disease, or replace emergency, primary, or ongoing medical care. It is designed to organize wellness information and support better provider conversations.

Will joining Emerald Wellness automatically get me a prescription?

No. Prescription decisions require qualified licensed provider review where available, legally permitted, and clinically appropriate. Access, eligibility, and prescribing rules may vary by location.

Can Emerald Wellness help me prepare for a provider visit?

Yes. Members can organize intake answers, labs, medications, supplements, peptides, symptoms, wearable insights, and provider-review questions so conversations can begin with clearer context.

Why do services vary by location?

Healthcare, pharmacy, lab, prescription, and product rules are not the same everywhere. Provider availability, pharmacies, labs, product categories, and member pathways may vary based on state, local rules, and eligibility.

How should I use lab and biomarker information?

Use lab and biomarker information for organization, trend awareness, and provider conversations. Interpretation, diagnosis, and treatment decisions should be reviewed with a qualified healthcare provider.

Can Emerald Wellness organize my supplements, medications, peptides, and timing?

Yes. Emerald Wellness can help organize stack details, timing notes, interaction questions, and bloodwork context for education and provider review. It does not make medical decisions or replace professional guidance.

How does Emerald Wellness approach privacy and security?

Health information workflows should be handled with appropriate privacy, security, consent, and applicable legal requirements. Members should not use non-emergency tools for urgent or time-sensitive medical needs.

What should I do in an emergency?

Call 911 or your local emergency services immediately. Emerald Wellness is not an emergency service and should not be used for urgent medical situations.

What information should I keep updated?

Members should keep medications, supplements, peptides, allergies, symptoms, labs, provider notes, and major health changes as current as possible so their organized wellness record is more useful for review.

Can provider review change my plan?

Yes. A qualified provider may recommend changes, decline a requested pathway, request more information, or advise follow-up care based on clinical judgment, legal requirements, and individual eligibility.

This page is informational and not legal or medical advice. Consult qualified legal and healthcare professionals for formal compliance decisions.
